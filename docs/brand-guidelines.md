## 1. Brand Identity

### 1.1 Brand Name

**VisionNetworking** — always written as one word with a capital V and capital N. Never hyphenated. Never abbreviated in external communications except for the logomark "VN."

Acceptable forms:

- VisionNetworking (preferred)
- VN (logomark and informal internal use only)

Unacceptable forms:

- Vision Networking (two words)
- Vision-Networking (hyphenated)
- Visionnetworking (lowercase N)
- VN without the full name present elsewhere on the page

### 1.2 Tagline Options

Select the tagline that best fits the context and audience. All four are approved for use:

| Tagline | Best Used For |
|---|---|
| **"Iraq's Largest Supplier Network"** | Hero sections, general marketing, brand awareness campaigns |
| **"From BOQ to Delivery. Powered by AI."** | Product-focused pages, technical audiences, procurement officers |
| **"100+ Brands. 38 Buildings. One Platform."** | Social media, ads, quick-impact formats where scale matters |
| **"Connecting Iraq's Construction Supply Chain"** | B2B partnerships, investor materials, formal contexts |

### 1.3 Brand Personality

VisionNetworking presents itself through five personality pillars. Every piece of content should reflect at least two of these:

| Pillar | What It Means in Practice |
|---|---|
| **Professional** | Clean layouts, precise language, no slang. We take our clients' projects seriously. |
| **Trustworthy** | Transparent pricing, real data, verified suppliers. No exaggeration in claims. |
| **Innovative** | AI-powered quotations, smart matching, modern UI. We lead with technology. |
| **Fast** | Emphasis on speed — fast quotes, fast responses, fast delivery coordination. |
| **Iraqi-First** | We are built in Iraq, for Iraq. Iraqi suppliers, Iraqi context, Iraqi pride. |

### 1.4 Voice and Tone

**Voice** (consistent across all content):
- Confident but never arrogant
- Technical but always clear
- Authoritative without being cold
- Direct — get to the point

**Tone** (adjusts by context):

| Context | Tone Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Website copy | Confident, benefit-driven, concise |
| Social media | Energetic, proud, slightly informal |
| Email to clients | Warm, professional, solution-oriented |
| Investor/partner materials | Data-driven, authoritative, formal |
| Support communications | Patient, helpful, reassuring |
| Internal communications | Casual, collaborative, transparent |

**Language Guidelines:**

- Write in English for international audiences. Use Arabic for Iraqi-market campaigns and materials.
- When writing in Arabic, use Modern Standard Arabic for formal materials and Iraqi dialect only for social media when appropriate.
- Avoid jargon unless speaking to a technical audience (procurement officers, engineers). When used, define terms on first use.
- Use active voice. "VisionNetworking connects you to 100+ suppliers" not "You are connected to 100+ suppliers by VisionNetworking."
- Numbers and data strengthen trust. Use them: "38 buildings," "100+ brands," "AI-powered," "seconds, not days."

## 2. Visual Identity

### 2.1 Color Palette

#### Primary Colors

These three colors form the core of the VisionNetworking identity. Navy anchors the brand, Blue drives action, and Light Blue accents and highlights.

| Color | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Navy** | `#0F172A` | 15, 23, 42 | Primary backgrounds, headers, text, footer, logo background |
| **Blue** | `#0369A1` | 3, 105, 161 | Primary buttons, links, active states, key UI elements |
| **Light Blue** | `#0EA5E9` | 14, 165, 233 | Accents, hover states, highlights, progress indicators, icons |

#### Secondary / Neutral Colors

| Color | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **White** | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | Page backgrounds, card backgrounds, text on dark surfaces |
| **Light Grey** | `#F8FAFC` | 248, 250, 252 | Section backgrounds, alternating rows, subtle separation |
| **Border Grey** | `#E2E8F0` | 226, 232, 240 | Borders, dividers, input field outlines, card edges |

#### Semantic Colors (for UI states)

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Success Green** | `#16A34A` | Confirmations, success messages, completed states |
| **Warning Amber** | `#D97706` | Warnings, pending states, attention-needed indicators |
| **Error Red** | `#DC2626` | Errors, destructive actions, validation failures |
| **Info Blue** | `#0EA5E9` | Informational messages (uses Light Blue) |

#### Color Ratios

For any given layout, aim for approximately:

- **60%** Navy + White (dominant, grounding)
- **30%** Blue (action, emphasis)
- **10%** Light Blue + accent colors (highlights, personality)

#### Accessibility

- Always maintain WCAG 2.1 AA contrast ratios (minimum 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text).
- White text on Navy: 15.4:1 ratio (passes AAA).
- White text on Blue: 4.6:1 ratio (passes AA).
- Navy text on White: 15.4:1 ratio (passes AAA).
- Never place Light Blue text on White backgrounds — insufficient contrast. Use Blue or Navy instead.

### 2.3 Logo

#### Primary Logo

The VisionNetworking logo consists of the letters **"VN"** set in Poppins Bold, enclosed in a **navy rounded rectangle** with a corner radius of 12px. The letters are white.

| Variant | Background | Letters | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Navy (#0F172A) | White (#FFFFFF) | Default — use on light backgrounds |
| **Reversed** | White (#FFFFFF) | Navy (#0F172A) | Use on dark or photo backgrounds |
| **Monochrome Dark** | Transparent | Navy (#0F172A) | Documents, watermarks on light backgrounds |
| **Monochrome Light** | Transparent | White (#FFFFFF) | Overlays on dark images or video |

#### Logo Clear Space

Maintain a minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of the letter "V" in the logomark on all four sides. No text, graphics, or other visual elements may enter this space.

#### Minimum Size

- **Digital:** 32px height minimum
- **Print:** 10mm height minimum

#### Logo Misuse (Do Not)

- Do not stretch, skew, or rotate the logo
- Do not change the logo colors outside of approved variants
- Do not add drop shadows, outlines, or effects
- Do not place the primary logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
- Do not recreate the logo in a different typeface
- Do not animate the logo without brand team approval

### 2.4 Iconography

**Icon Library:** Lucide Icons (open source, consistent with modern UI standards)

**Style Rules:**

- Stroke weight: 1.5px (consistent across all icons)
- Size: 24px default, 20px for compact UI, 16px for inline text
- Color: Match surrounding text color, or use Blue (#0369A1) for interactive elements
- No filled icons — stroke-only for consistency
- Rounded line caps and joins

**Icon Usage by Context:**

| Context | Example Icons |
|---|---|
| Navigation | Menu, Search, User, Bell, Settings |
| Actions | Plus, Edit, Trash, Download, Send |
| Status | CheckCircle, AlertTriangle, XCircle, Clock |
| Domain | Building2, Package, FileText, Calculator, Truck |

| **Local expertise** | Built in Iraq, for Iraq. We understand local supply chains, brands, pricing dynamics, and delivery realities. |

These are the facts that set VisionNetworking apart. Use them consistently across all materials:

1. **Scale:** 38 buildings, 100+ brands — the largest supplier network in Iraq on a single platform.
2. **AI-Powered Quotations:** Upload a BOQ, receive matched supplier quotes automatically. No other Iraqi platform does this.

4. **B2B and B2C:** Serves both large contractors and individual homeowners — the full spectrum.

## 4. Photography & Imagery Direction

### 4.1 What to Use

All imagery should feel **authentic, local, and professional.** The goal is to reflect the real Iraqi construction and supply chain ecosystem — not a sanitized Western version of it.

**Preferred imagery:**

- **Real Iraqi buildings and construction sites.** Show actual projects — high-rises in Baghdad, residential developments in Erbil, commercial builds in Basra. Raw and in-progress is fine; it signals authenticity.
- **Real products in real showrooms.** Tiles, fixtures, electrical equipment, steel — photographed in actual supplier showrooms or warehouses in Iraq. Natural lighting preferred.
- **People at work.** Engineers reviewing plans, procurement officers on laptops, workers on site, suppliers in their showrooms. Faces build trust.
- **Diverse Iraqi representation.** Reflect the diversity of Iraq's business landscape — different regions, ages, and roles. Include both men and women in professional settings.
- **The platform in action.** Screenshots, screen recordings, and device mockups showing the VisionNetworking interface. These should always use real (or realistic) data, not placeholder lorem ipsum.
- **Aerial and wide shots of Iraqi cities.** For hero sections, backgrounds, and mood-setting. Show the scale of Iraq's construction boom.

**Photography style:**

- Natural lighting preferred over studio lighting
- Warm color temperature (reflecting Iraqi sunlight and warmth)
- Slight desaturation for a professional tone — avoid oversaturated, HDR-style processing
- Shallow depth of field for portrait/detail shots
- Wide or medium shots for contextual/environmental imagery

### 4.2 What to Avoid

- **Generic Western stock photography.** No smiling businesspeople in suits shaking hands in glass-walled offices that are clearly not in Iraq.
- **Overly polished, sterile imagery.** Construction is messy. Supply chains are complex. Imagery that looks too clean feels dishonest.
- **AI-generated images of people.** They undermine the trust we are building. If a human face is shown, it must be a real photograph.
- **Clip art or cartoon illustrations** for anything client-facing. Simple line illustrations (in the Lucide icon style) are acceptable for diagrams and infographics only.
- **Images that misrepresent scale.** Do not use imagery of mega-projects or international skylines to imply capabilities we do not yet have.
- **Culturally insensitive imagery.** Be aware of local norms. When in doubt, consult with the Iraqi team.
- **Watermarked or unlicensed images.** All imagery must be either original, properly licensed, or from verified free-use sources.

### 4.3 Image Treatment

When images are used as backgrounds or hero sections:

- Apply a Navy (#0F172A) overlay at 60-70% opacity for text legibility
- White text on overlaid images
- Ensure text passes WCAG AA contrast requirements even on the lightest area of the image
- Use CSS `object-fit: cover` for responsive cropping — never stretch images

## 5. Templates Direction

### 5.1 Business Card

**Layout:**

- **Front:** Navy (#0F172A) background. VN logomark top-left, small. Person's name in Poppins SemiBold 11pt, white. Title in Open Sans Regular 9pt, Light Blue (#0EA5E9). Positioned lower-third of the card.
- **Back:** White background. Full "VisionNetworking" wordmark centered, Navy. Below it, the tagline "Iraq's Largest Supplier Network" in Open Sans Regular 8pt, Blue (#0369A1). Contact details bottom-left in Open Sans 8pt: phone, email, website. QR code bottom-right linking to the platform or contact's profile.
- **Size:** Standard 3.5" x 2" (89mm x 51mm)
- **Finish:** Matte with spot UV on the VN logomark (front) and wordmark (back)
- **Paper:** 400gsm uncoated or soft-touch laminate

### 5.2 Email Signature

```
[Full Name]
[Job Title] | VisionNetworking
[Phone Number] | [Email Address]
www.visionnetworking.io

Iraq's Largest Supplier Network
```

**Formatting rules:**

- Name: Poppins SemiBold or bold system font, Navy (#0F172A)
- Title and details: Open Sans Regular or system font, dark grey (#334155)
- Tagline: Open Sans Regular italic, Blue (#0369A1)
- VN logomark (32px) to the left of the text block, vertically centered
- No banners, promotional images, or animated elements in signatures
- Maximum 6 lines including whitespace

### 5.3 Social Media Post Templates

#### Instagram / Facebook (Square 1080x1080)

**Template A — Data Point:**
- Navy background, full bleed
- Large number or statistic in Poppins Bold, White (e.g., "100+" or "38")
- Supporting text in Open Sans Regular, Light Blue
- VN logomark bottom-right corner, small
- Use for: brand awareness, milestone announcements, market stats

**Template B — Product/Supplier Spotlight:**
- Product or showroom photo, full bleed
- Navy gradient overlay from bottom (transparent to 90% opacity)
- Supplier name in Poppins SemiBold, White, positioned bottom-left
- Brand/product details in Open Sans, Light Blue, below the name
- VN logomark bottom-right corner
- Use for: supplier features, new brand onboarding, product highlights

**Template C — Testimonial / Quote:**
- White background
- Large quotation mark in Light Blue, decorative, top-left
- Quote text in Poppins Regular, Navy, centered
- Attribution in Open Sans SemiBold, Blue, below the quote
- Thin Blue line divider between quote and attribution
- VN logomark bottom-center
- Use for: client testimonials, team quotes, industry insights

**Template D — Platform Feature:**
- Split layout: left half Navy, right half a platform screenshot or mockup
- Feature name in Poppins Bold, White, on the navy half
- Brief description in Open Sans Regular, Light Blue, below
- CTA text in Open Sans SemiBold, White, with a subtle arrow icon
- Use for: feature launches, product updates, how-to previews

#### LinkedIn (1200x627)

- Follow the same template concepts as above but in landscape format
- Increase text size by approximately 20% for readability in the LinkedIn feed
- Always include the full "VisionNetworking" wordmark (not just VN) on LinkedIn posts

#### Stories (1080x1920)

- Bold, high-contrast layouts
- One message per story slide — do not overcrowd
- Use the type scale aggressively: large Poppins headings, minimal body text
- Swipe-up or link sticker area must be kept clear (bottom 15% of the frame)

### 5.4 Presentation Slides

**Format:** 16:9 widescreen (1920x1080)

**Title Slide:**
- Navy background, full bleed
- VisionNetworking wordmark, top-left, white
- Presentation title in Poppins Bold 44pt, white, centered vertically
- Subtitle/date in Open Sans Regular 20pt, Light Blue, below the title
- Thin horizontal Light Blue line between title and subtitle

**Section Divider Slide:**
- Navy background
- Section number in Poppins Bold 120pt, Light Blue, low opacity (20%), positioned as a large background element
- Section title in Poppins SemiBold 36pt, white, centered

**Content Slide:**
- White background
- Slide title in Poppins SemiBold 28pt, Navy, top-left
- Body text in Open Sans Regular 18pt, dark grey (#334155)
- Key data or callouts in Blue (#0369A1) or Light Blue (#0EA5E9)
- VN logomark bottom-right, small, Navy, on every slide
- Maximum 6 bullet points per slide. If you need more, split across two slides.

**Data/Chart Slide:**
- White background
- Chart title in Poppins SemiBold 24pt, Navy
- Use the brand color palette for chart elements: Navy, Blue, Light Blue as primary series; use Border Grey for gridlines
- Source attribution in Open Sans 12pt, grey, bottom-left
- Avoid 3D charts, heavy gradients, or decorative chart elements

**Closing Slide:**
- Navy background
- "Thank You" or CTA in Poppins Bold 44pt, white, centered
- Contact details in Open Sans Regular 18pt, Light Blue, below
- VisionNetworking wordmark and tagline, bottom-center, white

**General Slide Rules:**
- No more than 3 colors per slide (choose from the brand palette)
- Generous whitespace — never fill more than 60% of a slide with content
- Left-align text unless centering is intentional (titles, CTAs)
- Use Lucide-style icons to support bullet points rather than generic bullet characters
- All images must follow the Photography & Imagery guidelines in Section 4
- No clip art, WordArt, or decorative fonts

## 6. Usage Rules & Governance

### 6.1 Brand Asset Access

All brand assets (logos, templates, color swatches, font files) should be stored in a shared, version-controlled location accessible to all authorized team members and partners. Distribute a brand kit (ZIP or shared drive folder) containing:

- Logo files in SVG, PNG (transparent), and PDF formats
- Color palette swatches (.ase for Adobe, .sketchpalette for Sketch/Figma)
- Font files or Google Fonts links
- Template files for presentations, social media, and business cards
- This brand guidelines document

### 6.2 Approval Process

- Any new external-facing material (ad, brochure, video, social post) must be reviewed against these guidelines before publication.
- Significant departures from the guidelines (new colors, alternate logos, different fonts) require approval from the brand owner or marketing lead.
- Supplier partners and agencies must receive a copy of this document and confirm adherence before producing materials on VisionNetworking's behalf.

### 6.3 Co-Branding

When VisionNetworking appears alongside a partner or supplier brand:

- The VisionNetworking logo must be at least equal in size to the partner logo
- Maintain full clear space around the VN logo — do not merge or overlap logos
- Use "in partnership with" or "powered by" language, not "a division of" or "by"
- Both brands' color palettes may coexist, but VisionNetworking's Navy should anchor the layout
